Good to know

Frequently asked questions.

What is opacity testing?
For 2012 and older diesel vehicles. It measures exhaust smoke using an SAE J1667 opacity meter to confirm the engine is burning clean enough to meet CARB limits.
What is the visual emissions inspection?
Required for 2012 and older diesel, and 2017 or older alt-fuel vehicles. We check that the emissions control equipment — DPF, EGR, SCR, NOx/O2 sensors, ECM and more — is present and in proper condition.
What is OBD testing?
For 2013+ diesel or 2018+ alt-fuel vehicles. We use a CARB-certified reader to verify monitor readiness and pull the emissions data your truck reports.
What does it cost?
Pricing is custom and depends on vehicle count, service type, and location. Request a quote and we'll get you a number quickly.
How do I avoid penalties?
Submit your tests before the deadline — you can test up to 90 days early. Late or missing tests can lead to DMV registration blocks and CARB citations.
What vehicle information do I need?
Just four things to start: VIN, license plate number, year, and fuel type.
I cleared my engine light — how do I meet the readiness criteria?
No DTCs or Permanent DTCs (PDTCs) can be present, and once codes are cleared (SCC) the vehicle must meet one of the following:

With no PDTC present

  • At least 5 warm-up cycles (WUC) since codes cleared, or
  • At least 200 miles travelled SCC, or
  • At least 400 minutes engine run time SCC, or
  • At most 2 incomplete OBD monitors.

If a PDTC is present (code is ignored when)

  • At least 15 WUC SCC, or
  • At least 400 miles travelled SCC, or
  • At least 800 minutes engine run time SCC, or
  • At most 1 incomplete OBD monitor.

An OBD test that doesn't meet the above gets a "Not Ready" result and won't satisfy the compliance requirement. If that happens, keep operating the vehicle normally for a few days, then have it retested.
